Grading

Assignment Parts and Points

Details on the specific parts and point values are provided in GradeScope.

Proposed Defective Solutions:

  • When identifying the principles that a defective solution has violated, you must clearly explain why you believe a principle is violated.
  • Within individual problems, to earn full credit, you must identify the single main problem in each flawed design. You may list several flaws, but there is usually one primary issue that should be identified.

Your Solution:

  • In each problem, your final design must address the issues you have identified and present a solution that is better than the problematic ones.
  • If your design is no better or is worse than the provided solutions, you will not earn full credit.
  • To earn full credit, you must submit a solution that improves at least one of the two provided solutions by correctly fixing the identified violations.

Design Problem 1 - Online Quizzes

Problem Statement

In an online quiz application, teachers update quizzes and students take them. Quizzes and questions have their own unique ID numbers. The same quiz question can appear in multiple quizzes. When a teacher updates the actual query made by a quiz question, the update should be reflected in all quizzes that utilize that question. A student takes a quiz by providing a quiz ID to the application at the time of taking the quiz.
